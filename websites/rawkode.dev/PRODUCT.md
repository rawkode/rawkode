# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

This site is for Rawkode's peers, fellow developers, conference organizers, open-source collaborators, and engineers exploring Rawkode Academy. They arrive to understand who David Flanagan is, inspect the apps and tools being built, read current thinking, find speaker details, and decide whether to go deeper into the Rawkode ecosystem.

## Product Purpose

rawkode.dev is David Flanagan's personal web presence and proof surface. It connects identity, open-source work, writing, speaker material, and Rawkode Academy in one place. Success means visitors can quickly understand Rawkode's focus, see real shipped work, trust the technical depth behind it, and follow a clear path to apps, writing, speaking details, or the Academy.

## Brand Personality

Tech, future-facing, sleek, and sharp. The interface should project confidence first, then reward curiosity through thoughtful details, strong hierarchy, polished interactions, and evidence of craft.

## Anti-references

Do not make this feel like a generic SaaS landing page, product dashboard, or developer-template portfolio. Avoid decorative technical cosplay: terminal gimmicks, purple gradient hero defaults, vague "developer aesthetic" shortcuts, endless card grids, and visual effects that compete with the actual work.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with substance. Apps, writing, biography details, and the Academy path should do the talking.
2. Prefer editorial rhythm over template grids. Use strong hierarchy, asymmetry, selective density, and breathing room to frame proof of craft.
3. Make technical work feel premium. Use precise typography, sharp interaction states, and restrained motion instead of common developer shortcuts.
4. Route attention deliberately. Guide visitors from identity to apps, from apps to writing, and from writing or biography to Rawkode Academy and contact paths.
5. Keep the system legible in both themes. Dark-first is the default, but light mode, contrast, focus states, and reduced-motion behavior must remain production-ready.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for body text, links, controls, focus states, and generated content. Preserve keyboard access with visible focus indicators and semantic navigation. Support reduced motion, avoid motion-gated content, keep line lengths readable, and maintain the existing light, dark, and system theme behavior.
