# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who use notes as a working database: daily note writers, builders, researchers, and operators who need notes, typed entities, sketches, and queryable views in one local-first workspace. They are usually inside an active workflow, capturing context, linking entities, refining schema, or pulling a result set out of their notes.

## Product Purpose

Notes is a local-first iOS and macOS app for daily notes, typed supertags, entity references, and query-driven views backed by SQLite. Success means a user can write naturally, promote structure when it becomes useful, edit schemas directly, and trust that dynamic views reflect real local data without turning the writing surface into an outliner.

## Brand Personality

Precise, quiet, capable. The product should feel like a serious personal knowledge tool: dense enough for repeated work, calm enough for writing, and explicit about schema/data changes.

## Anti-references

Do not copy an outliner-first Tana model where everything becomes nested nodes. Do not make Markdown pretend to be a database through fragile punctuation. Avoid marketing-style hero surfaces, decorative cards, oversized type, novelty controls, and settings screens that hide important schema consequences behind vague labels.

## Design Principles

- Daily notes are first-class workspace objects, not a folder convention.
- Structure is promoted deliberately: typed supertags, saved views, and schema fields should feel explicit and inspectable.
- Database editing must be direct, reversible, and understandable before the user commits a schema change.
- Use familiar product UI patterns for repeated work: sidebars, lists, forms, inspectors, inline validation, and compact controls.
- Preserve local-first trust: avoid hidden network assumptions, silent destructive updates, and ambiguous persistence states.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-accessible controls. Support reduced motion by keeping transitions functional rather than decorative. Do not rely on color alone for validation, destructive actions, or schema state.
